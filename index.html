Discover Peterborough

The Weston Homes Stadium
The Weston Homes Stadium

The stadium has a capacity of around 15,314 spectators and features both seating and standing areas.

It has undergone several renovations and expansions over the years to meet safety regulations and improve facilities for fans.

It has also hosted various events such as music concerts and charity matches.

LongThorpe Tower
LongThorpe Tower

It is known for its remarkable and well-preserved medieval wall paintings, which date back to the 14th century.

The tower was constructed in the late 13th century as a fortified tower house, likely as a residence for the de Thorpe family. It consists of three stories and a basement.

The paintings were rediscovered in the 1940s when the tower was being restored.
Burghley House
Burghley House

Burghley House has been the ancestral home of the Cecil family for over 400 years and is still privately owned.

The exterior features intricate stonework, tall chimneys, and ornate details, while the interior boasts grand halls, opulent chambers, and exquisite decorative elements.
Peterborough Lido
Peterborough Lido

Peterborough Lido, also known as the Peterborough Lido Outdoor Swimming Pool, is an open-air swimming pool located in Peterborough.

It is a popular recreational facility that offers a refreshing swimming experience during the summer months.

The Lido was originally opened in 1936 and has been a beloved destination for locals and visitors alike ever since.
Sacrewell Farm
Sacrewell Farm

It is a working farm that offers a range of activities and experiences for visitors of all ages.

The farm covers an expansive area and provides an opportunity for visitors to interact with and learn about various farm animals.
